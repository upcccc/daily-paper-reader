Abstract 

We investigate the integration of Nesterov-type acceleration into primalвАУdual methods for structured convex optimization. While proximal splitting algorithms efficiently handle composite problems of the form min x f (x) + g(x) + h(Kx ), accelerating their convergence with respect to the smooth term f is notoriously challenging due to the rotational dynamics in the primalвАУ dual space. In this paper, we overcome this barrier by proposing the Accelerated Proximal Alternating PredictorвАУCorrector algorithm (APAPC), focusing on the setting where g(x) =

Our analysis reveals that Nesterov momentum can be seamlessly integrated into a primalвАУdual forwardвАУbackward scheme by exploiting the strong convexity of the dual problem to stabilize the accelerated primal updates. Using a unified Lyapunov framework, we establish optimal O(1 /t 2) sublinear convergence rates, as well as accelerated linear convergence when 

ќЉg > 0, across three regimes of dual strong convexity: (i) when h is smooth, (ii) when the linear operator KвИЧ is bounded below, and (iii) for linearly constrained optimization. Furthermore, leveraging recent results on accelerated gradient descent, we characterize the weak convergence of the primalвАУdual iterates to a saddle-point solution.

In this work, we study the acceleration of PAPC , which has a primalвАУdual forwardвАУbackward struc-ture similar to CV , to solve Problem (1) under the specific assumption that g = ќЉg

Furthermore, allowing for a general g significantly complicates the landscape of convergence guarantees. For example, the linear convergence we establish in Section 3.4 for the linearly constrained setting cannot be achieved with an arbitrary function g, even if it is strongly convex [Alghunaim et al., 2021, Sec-tion VI]. Consequently, we defer the development of fully general accelerated algorithms to future work, and we focus on the structured setting with g = ќЉg

Our proposed APAPC substantially generalizes these previous efforts by accommodating an arbitrary nonsmooth composite term hвЧ¶K. We also acknowledge the concurrent work of Zhu [2025], who proposed the Accelerated PrimalвАУDual Fixed-Point method ( APDFP ). When ќЉg = 0 , their algorithm coincides with APAPC . # 4 Conclusion 

We introduced the Accelerated Proximal Alternating Predictor-Corrector algorithm ( APAPC ), suc-cessfully embedding Nesterov momentum into a fully split primalвАУdual method for structured con-vex optimization. By demonstrating that primal acceleration can be stabilized by exploiting the strong convexity of the dual problem, we established optimal O(1 /t 2) sublinear convergence rates, as well as accelerated linear convergence, across several problem regimes. Furthermore, leverag-ing our unified Lyapunov framework, we characterized the weak convergence of the primalвАУdual iterates. There are several promising avenues for future research. A natural extension is to generalize 

APAPC to solve Problem (1) with an arbitrary nonsmooth function g. However, while accelerated linear convergence is likely attainable when h is smooth (analogous to the results in Section 3.2), linear convergence will generally not hold for an arbitrary g in the settings of Sections 3.3 and 3.4 [Zhao, 2022]. Beyond deterministic optimization, future work will explore randomized variants of 

APAPC . The incorporation of stochastic gradient estimators [Zhao et al., 2019, Salim et al., 2022b, Zhao, 2022, Li et al., 2022, Ehrhardt et al., 2025] is of particular interest. Similarly, replacing exact proximal steps with stochastic or approximate evaluations [Chambolle et al., 2018, Condat and Richt√°rik, 2023, Condat et al., 2025, Combettes and Madariaga, 2025] represents a critical next step toward reducing the computational burden in large-scale inverse problems [Papoutsellis et al., 2025] and machine learning applications [Mishchenko et al., 2022, Condat et al., 2026]. 27 Acknowledgements
