Abstract 

As data-privacy regulations tighten and statistical models are increasingly deployed on sensitive human-sourced data, privacy-preserving linear regression has become a critical necessity. For the add-remove DP model, Kulesza et al. [2024] and Fitzsimons et al. [2024] have independently shown that the size of the dataset ‚ÄĒ an important statistic for linear regression ‚ÄĒ can be privately estimated for ‚Äėfree‚Äô, via a simplex transformation of bounded variables and private sum queries on the transformed variables. In this work, we extend this free lunch result via carefully crafted multidimensional simplex transformations to variables and functions that are bounded in the interval [0 , 1]. We show that these transformations can be applied to refine the estimates of sufficient statistics needed for private simple linear regression based on ordinary least squares. We provide both analytical and numerical results to demonstrate the superiority of our approach. Our proposed transformations have general applicability and can be readily adapted for differentially private polynomial regression. 

Keywords. differential privacy, linear regression, simplex transformation, variance reduction, sufficient statistics 

# 1 Introduction 

Differential privacy [Dwork et al., 2006] has become the gold standard for releasing statistical insights from sensitive datasets while providing rigorous privacy guarantees [Near et al., 2025]. In linear regression, the challenge is to privately estimate model parameters from data D = {(xi, y i)}ni=1 while maintaining utility. Traditional approaches [Alabi et al., 2022] privatize sufficient statistics independently, adding noise scaled to individual sensitivities. Various techniques have been developed to improve upon basic Laplace mechanism, including concentrated differential privacy [Bun and Steinke, 2016], smooth sensitivity methods [Bun and Steinke, 2019], and robust estimation approaches [Kamath et al., 2020, Liu et al., 2021]. While effective, these methods suffer from high variance accumulation [Cai et al., 2021, Kamath et al., 2023], particularly in stringent privacy regimes or with small sample sizes. Recent work by Kulesza et al. [2024] and Fitzsimons et al. [2024] revealed a key insight: for bounded data, the dataset size n can be estimated ‚Äúfor free‚ÄĚ via simplex transformations without additional privacy cost. In this paper, we extend this free lunch result to construct multiple independent unbiased estimators 

for each sufficient statistic in simple linear regression based on ordinary least squares. By applying inverse-variance weighting, we achieve substantial variance reduction‚ÄĒup to 4 .8√ó for critical quadratic statistics while maintaining identical privacy guarantees. 1

# 3 Background and Related Work 

## 3.1 The Free Lunch via Simplex Transformations 

For univariate bounded data x ‚ąą [0 , 1], the simplex transformation x 7 ‚Üí (x, 1 ‚ąí x) enables a remarkable result [Kulesza et al., 2024, Fitzsimons et al., 2024]: privatizing the sum pair ( Sx, S 1‚ąíx) with Laplace noise Lap(1 /őĶ ) yields both a noisy sum ňúSx and a ‚Äúfree‚ÄĚ size estimate ňú n = ( ňúSx + ňúS1‚ąíx). The variance of ňúSx remains identical to direct privatization, so ňú n incurs zero additional privacy cost. This generalizes to any bounded function f (x) ‚ąą [0 , 1]: the sum pair ( Sf , S 1‚ąíf ) has joint ‚Ąď1-sensitivity 1, enabling private size estimation. The key is that the complementary components sum to a constant n,creating an algebraic constraint that can be exploited via post-processing. 

## 3.2 Prior Work on Private Linear Regression 

Traditional DP linear regression methods privatize sufficient statistics independently. For simple linear regression requiring {Sx, S y , S x2 , S xy }, standard approaches allocate privacy budget equally and add in-dependent noise to each statistic [Alabi et al., 2022]. Under the swap model (replacing one record with another), Alabi et al. [2022] achieved strong results by exploiting the known dataset size. In another related work, Amin et al. [2023] suggested to partition the dataset into roughly m = 1000 subsets, non-privately estimate a regression model on each, and then apply the exponential mechanism to privately estimate a model; however, such an approach may not be feasible for small to moderate sized datasets. A related line of work is explored in Ferrando and Sheldon [2025], who proposed private regression via data-dependent sufficient statistic perturbation; their approach, however, is restricted only to discrete data whereas our method operates directly on continuous data in [0 , 1]. Another prominent approach is Differentially Private Stochastic Gradient Descent (DP-SGD) [Bassily et al., 2014, Abadi et al., 2016, Cai et al., 2021]. These approaches typically require careful hyperparam-eter tuning, and some open-source implementations have been found to have overestimated their privacy guarantees [Annamalai et al., 2025, Chua et al., 2024]. The add/remove model where neighboring datasets differ by addition or removal of a record imposes stricter requirements. Dataset size becomes unknown and must itself be estimated privately. This model is more challenging but provides stronger privacy guarantees [Near et al., 2025]. For comparison with prior art, we adapt the two baseline methods to the add/remove setting: DP-SS (Differentially Private Sufficient Statistics) [Alabi et al., 2022] and DP-Theil-Sen mechanisms [Dwork and Lei, 2009, Alabi et al., 2022]. In this paper, we extend the free lunch insight for add/remove model beyond simple size estimation. We show that carefully designed multidimensional simplex transformations enable constructing multiple independent estimators for the same statistic, which can be optimally combined to dramatically reduce variance without consuming additional privacy budget. 3Accepted at the SeQureDB Workshop at ACM SIGMOD 2026 and TPDP Workshop 2026

# 7 Conclusion 

In this work, we introduced DP-RSS, a novel mechanism for differentially private linear regression in the add/remove DP model. By exploiting the algebraic structure of bounded data and applying novel simplex transformations, we derived multiple independent estimators for key sufficient statistics without consuming additional privacy budget. Our theoretical analysis and empirical evaluations demonstrate that DP-RSS consistently outperforms the standard DP-SS estimator. Further, compared to the DP-Theil-Sen estimator, our experimental results highlight a significant reduction in error with our DP-RSS mechanism in the high-privacy (low őĶ) regime. Finally, we remark that our multidimensional simplex transformation can be readily applied to the adaptive framework presented in Wang [2018, Alg. 2], and can further improve the performance of the BinAgg algorithm [Lin et al., 2026] via refined estimates of private sums. 

Future Work. Several promising directions remain open: (1) Optimal privacy allocation for polynomial regression : While our approach naturally extends to degree-d polynomials, determining the optimal split of privacy budget őĶ = őĶ1 + őĶ2 among the O(d) statistics to minimize regression error is an important open problem. (2) Multivariate regression : Extending our simplex transformation techniques to multiple input variables while maintaining computational efficiency. (3) Other statistical tasks : Exploring whether similar algebraic constraints can be leveraged for variance reduction in other estimation problems under add/remove differential privacy, such as generalized linear models or robust regression. 13 Accepted at the SeQureDB Workshop at ACM SIGMOD 2026 and TPDP Workshop 2026
